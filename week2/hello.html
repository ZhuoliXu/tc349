Critique:
The home page tries to make the website clean, neat and simple, so there are only some big icons listing here. However, though it looks clear, it does not look good on screen, because it lacks of balance, especially for those users with low-resolution monitors. Another bad thing here is, users did not know what these icons stand for. They do not even know if they be clicked or not. Not like a "house" icon, we all know it stands for home page. I suggested to use the traditional navigation bar to replace this.

Another thing this website should change is its stupid sloagan, like "We make super nice apps", "The best usenet browser in the world", "A very nice SSH client", etc. Yes indeed you need to market yourself, but not this way. Think about your users. Does my mom need to use SSH client? Does my accounting major girlfriend know what FTP is? User are professional, or to be prfessional. Just remove those baroque words and list their basic functions, and add a "more" button to let users want to enter in.
